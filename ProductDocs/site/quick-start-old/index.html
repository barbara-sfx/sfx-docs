Sign up or sign in

  • If your team already has an account, ask your admin for an invitation, then sign in.

  • Otherwise, go to the SignalFx website and sign up for our free trial.

Send in metrics

  • If you are already familiar with metrics and just want to know how to configure your existing collectors to send data to SignalFx, check out our documentation on sending metrics.

  • If you have instances on AWS (or can get them easily), then integrate with Amazon Web Services to pull in CloudWatch metrics.

  • You can also install our streamlined default configuration of the popular open source agent, collectd.

  • Finally, you can send in custom application metrics or use our API.

Create a chart or two

  • Once you have metrics flowing into SignalFx, it's time to create your first chart.
  • If you'd like to understand how our SignalFlow analytics work, create your create your first analytics pipeline.

Go deep on monitoring and analytics

  • Invite your teammates to join you. Build dashboards that help you understand how your services and systems affect one another. Work together better across teams.
  • Send in metrics that matter to your application, your infrastructure and your business. Explore the different ways you can do this and make sure you're choosing the best metric types for your data.
  • Understand how the parts of the SignalFx application work together to let you create and manage charts, by reading up on charts, dashboards and dashboard groups, and the Catalog.
  • Set up detectors and alerts on your most important metrics so that you can be notified if anything goes wrong.
  • Go deep on how the SignalFlow analytics engine works and what functions it makes available. Add metadata to your metrics to get the most out of our visualization and analytics.
  • Dig into our best practices around how to monitor elastic infrastructure like AWS, and come up with some of your own.

Become an active member of our community

  • Got a story to share? Best practices that would benefit others on how to make the best use of analytics in monitoring? Get in touch with us

  • Contribute to our open source collector efforts on GitHub and help others collect, aggregate and send the metrics and events they need. While you're there, check out the exciting proijects in our other repos.
